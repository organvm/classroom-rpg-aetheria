**Rules & Protocols for the Macro Schedule**  
  
*This fully revised list ensures that all refinements are locked into the course structure.*  
  
📖** 1. Structural Framework (Macro-Level)**  
  
✅ **Each section builds on the last** (*micro-level: section-to-section logic*).  
✅ **Each week connects and escalates concepts** (*daily structure*).  
✅ **Each week builds logically upon the last** (*macro-level: week-to-week continuity*).  
✅ **The entire course moves toward a larger goal** (*meta-level: semester-long cohesion*).  
✅ **Each transition ties together past discussions, present objectives, and future learning.**  
✅ **Weeks must be organized by time, place, and movement** (*instead of focusing on individual authors*).  
✅ **Literary movements must explicitly show the evolution of literary form (oral → verse → prose → modern hybrid forms).**  
  
📖** 2. Standardized Weekly Format**  
  
*Every week in the schedule must include the following elements:*  
  
✅ **Historical Anchor** (*Framing the literary movement within a major historical shift*).  
✅ **Three Connected Events (Far Past, Present, Distant Future)** (*A historical event, a contemporary moment, and a speculative future tie-in*).  
✅ **Themes & Core Questions** (*What big ideas are being explored?*).  
✅ **Literary Movement** (*Clearly defined and contextualized*).  
✅ **Key Readings** (*Selections from the movement, no full novels*).  
✅ **Key Literary Terms** (*Explicitly stated and applied in discussions and assignments*).  
✅ **Practical Applications** (*Three categories: Work, Home, Universal*).  
✅ **Healthcare/Work-Related Question** (*An interdisciplinary, real-world application, including law, AI, ethics, and other fields*).  
✅ **Visual Art & Film** (*5 pieces of visual art + 3 films spanning different decades*).  
✅ **Structured Film/Art Analysis Activity** (*Students must directly analyze visual storytelling techniques*).  
✅ **Assignments** (*Journals, discussions, close reading, final research paper in Weeks 13-15*).  
  
📖** 3. Historical & Cultural Anchoring**  
  
✅ **Each week must be tied to a specific time and place** (*instead of isolating texts or authors*).  
✅ **Eastern & Western traditions must be compared based on the state of their cultures and interactions at that time.**  
✅ **Philosophy, history, and art must be integrated with literature** (*not treated as separate elements*).  
  
📖** 4. Work & Interdisciplinary Integration**  
  
✅ **Each week must explicitly include a work/healthcare-related question.**  
✅ **Themes must be applied to real-world problems (bias in diagnosis, AI-generated therapy, unreliable memory in forensic testimony, etc.).**  
✅ **Every interdisciplinary connection must include healthcare as well as other relevant fields (law, AI, history, ethics, cognitive science, etc.).**  
  
📖** 5. Visual & Media Integration**  
  
✅ **Each week must include:**  
	•	**5 pieces of visual art (varied mediums)**.  
	•	**3 films spanning different decades (25-50 years apart)**.  
✅ **Art & film must be actively discussed, not just listed.**  
✅ **Students must engage in structured comparison activities between visual, literary, and cinematic narratives.**  
✅ **At least one structured film/art analysis per week (not passive viewing).**  
  
📖** 6. Literary Analysis & Research Progression**  
  
✅ **Students must analyze texts BEFORE reading them.**  
✅ **Themes must be introduced broadly before students apply them to specific works.**  
✅ **Week 1 must focus on setting up literary analysis skills & structuring literary narratives.**  
✅ **Writing & research skill-building must be scaffolded throughout the semester (annotated bibliographies, source analysis, literary argumentation).**  
✅ **Weeks 13-15 must be fully dedicated to the final research paper (proposal, drafting, peer review, revision).**  
  
📖** 7. Transitions & Continuity**  
  
✅ **Each transition must explicitly connect the past, present, and future themes of the class.**  
✅ **Transitions should remind students what they’ve already learned and prepare them for what’s coming next.**  
✅ **Key words & phrases should repeat between sections to recontextualize ideas.**  
✅ **No section stands alone—each builds into the next, both within the day and across weeks.**  
  
📖** 8. Discussion & Question Formatting**  
  
✅ **Each discussion must include:**  
	•	**Concrete Questions (Observation-Based)** (*Fact-based, directly from the text.*).  
	•	**Abstract Questions (Concept-Based)** (*Why does this matter? What broader themes or ideas emerge?*).  
	•	**Connecting Questions (Application-Based)** (*How does this text relate to other works, real life, or modern media?*).  
	•	**Exploratory Questions (Challenging & Speculative)** (*What if the story had unfolded differently? How might another writer approach this same concept?*).  
  
📖** 9. Final Research Paper Integration**  
  
✅ **The last two weeks of the course (Weeks 13-15) must be fully dedicated to the research paper.**  
✅ **Paper progression:**  
	•	**Week 13:** Research Proposal & Peer Review  
	•	**Week 14:** Drafting & Instructor Feedback  
	•	**Week 15:** Final Submission & Reflection  
✅ **Students must apply movement-based literary analysis in their final paper.**  
  
📖** 10. Error Checking & Review Protocol**  
  
✅ **Before finalizing a week’s schedule:**  
	•	**Double-check that all sections appear in logical order.**  
	•	**Ensure no content has been omitted from the original compiled schedule.**  
	•	**Confirm transitions effectively connect all elements.**  
	•	**Verify discussion & question formatting is consistent throughout.**  
	•	**Ensure practical applications and skill-based assignments are included.**
