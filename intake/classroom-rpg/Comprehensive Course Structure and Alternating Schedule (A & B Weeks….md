🔄 A & B Week Alternation: Class Structure Differences  
  

| Schedule Component | A Week (Discussion-Heavy) | B Week (Close Reading-Heavy) |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Opening Discussion & Work/Healthcare Application | Student-Led Case Study on real-world application. | Instructor-guided discussion on thematic connections between literature and the real world. |
| Historical Anchor & Core Questions | Open-ended discussion to explore cultural & historical framing. | Close textual analysis of primary sources from the historical era that inform the literature. |
| Personal Lens (Daily Journal & Meta-Reflection) | Pet Peeve & Reflection Prompt (Focus on narrative, self-awareness, perception). | Applied Reflection: How does this week’s reading challenge or affirm your worldview? |
| The Medium is the Message | Technology & Media Influence Lecture (How new media altered storytelling). | Comparative Analysis (How different media represent the same ideas). |
| Building the Literary Toolkit (Key Terms & Structures) | Focus on genre, movement, and structure. | Hands-on Annotation (Identifying and applying literary terms in readings). |
| Storytelling Across Mediums (Visual Art, Film, Interdisciplinary) | Discussion on Art & Film Connections to the text. | Comparing Passages & Scenes from films, paintings, and literature. |
| Bridging Ideas: Applying Past Concepts to New Readings | Backward Application: How do past weeks’ readings inform this week’s themes? | Forward Projection: How does this week’s literature set up future texts? |
| Sharpening the Craft (Proactive Writing Tip) | Discussion-Based Writing Tip (Workshop Style) | Annotation & Argumentation Tip (Applied Exercise) |
| Guided Close Reading & Inquiry | Focus on broad thematic questions leading into the close reading homework. | Deep textual analysis session for that week’s close reading assignment. |
| Looking Forward (Prep for Homework & Readings) | Preview next week’s concepts & provide reading strategies. | Ensure students leave with clear takeaways for their analysis paper. |
| Independent Inquiry & Research Task | Open research session or creative reflection | Guided literary analysis session to prepare for the upcoming assignments. |
  
  
✅ Why This Works:  
	•	Alternating Discussion Weeks (A) and Close Reading Weeks (B) balances engagement styles.  
	•	Ensures literary analysis remains grounded in real-world contexts.  
	•	Scaffolds writing & reading skills progressively across weeks.
