# Finalized 15-Week Macro Schedule (Literary Movements & Protocols Integrated)  
  
📖 UNIT 1: Foundations of Literary Analysis (Weeks 1-2)  
  
Week 1 introduces students to literary analysis, storytelling, and the power of narrative. Week 2 moves into epic narratives and the oral tradition.  
  
Week 1: The Literary Narrative & Why Stories Matter  
	•	Themes: Why do humans tell stories? What is the role of literature in shaping society?  
	•	Core Questions:  
	•	What is the function of storytelling across cultures?  
	•	How do we analyze literature?  
	•	What makes a text “literary”?  
	•	Reading Selections:  
	•	Plato, The Allegory of the Cave  
	•	Ursula K. Le Guin, Telling is Listening  
	•	Emily Dickinson, Selected Poems  
	•	Jorge Luis Borges, The Library of Babel  
	•	Literary Terms: Allegory, Metaphor, Symbolism, Narrative Perspective  
	•	Assignments: Daily Journal, Discussion Post, Close Reading Assignment  
	•	Visual Art & Film: Early cave paintings, Borges & AI-generated literature  
  
Week 2: The Oral Tradition & Epic Narratives (1200 BCE–500 CE)  
	•	Western: The Iliad, The Odyssey, The Aeneid  
	•	Eastern: The Mahabharata, The Ramayana, The Tale of the Heike  
	•	Themes: Heroism, fate, gods vs. humanity, oral storytelling & memory  
	•	Historical Context:  
	•	Greek & Roman oral poetry vs. Indian and Japanese epics  
	•	The transition from oral to written storytelling  
	•	Assignments: Discussion on Heroism, Comparative Analysis  
  
📖 UNIT 2: Religious Texts, Philosophy & Medieval Allegory (Weeks 3-5)  
  
Week 3: Sacred Texts & The Birth of Moral Storytelling (500 BCE–1300 CE)  
	•	Western: The Bible (Genesis, Job), Dante’s Inferno, Beowulf  
	•	Eastern: The Bhagavad Gita, Tao Te Ching, The Qur’an (Selections)  
	•	Themes: Morality, divine justice, the hero’s journey, cosmic balance  
	•	Historical Context:  
	•	The role of sacred texts in literature & law  
	•	Medieval Christian allegory vs. Eastern philosophical poetry  
	•	Assignments: Moral Dilemmas in Literature, Symbolism in Religious Texts  
	•	Visual Art: Illuminated manuscripts, Persian miniatures, Buddhist temple murals  
  
Week 4: Chivalry, Courtly Love & The Medieval Imagination (1100–1500 CE)  
	•	Western: The Canterbury Tales, Sir Gawain & The Green Knight, Arthurian Legends  
	•	Eastern: The Tale of Genji (Japan), The Conference of the Birds (Persia)  
	•	Themes: Honor, romance, destiny, satire, social structure  
	•	Historical Context:  
	•	European feudalism & the knightly code vs. samurai culture in Japan  
	•	The influence of Islamic literature on European storytelling  
	•	Assignments: Close Reading on Gender & Power, Storytelling Structures  
  
📖 UNIT 3: Renaissance, Enlightenment & The Rise of the Novel (Weeks 6-8)  
  
Week 5: The Renaissance & The Individual (1300–1600 CE)  
	•	Western: Hamlet, Don Quixote, Paradise Lost  
	•	Eastern: Ming Dynasty Poetry, Noh Drama (Japan)  
	•	Themes: Selfhood, exploration, religious doubt, art & creativity  
	•	Historical Context:  
	•	Humanism & the printing press  
	•	Divergence of European & Chinese literary traditions  
	•	Assignments: Analysis of the Self in Renaissance Texts, Discussion on Rebellion  
  
Week 6: The Age of Reason & Social Satire (1600–1800 CE)  
	•	Western: Candide, Gulliver’s Travels, Rousseau’s Confessions  
	•	Eastern: The Dream of the Red Chamber (China), Basho’s Haiku (Japan)  
	•	Themes: Rationality vs. emotion, satire, democracy & revolution  
	•	Assignments: Satire in Literature & Politics, Close Reading of Irony  
  
📖 UNIT 4: Romanticism, Realism & Psychological Exploration (Weeks 9-11)  
  
Week 7: Romanticism & The Gothic (1780–1850 CE)  
	•	Western: Frankenstein, The Rime of the Ancient Mariner, Goethe’s Faust  
	•	Eastern: Strange Tales from a Chinese Studio (China), Japanese Ghost Stories  
	•	Themes: Emotion over reason, nature & the supernatural, rebellion  
	•	Assignments: Close Reading on the Sublime, The Psychology of Fear  
  
Week 8: Realism, Naturalism & The Search for Truth (1850–1900 CE)  
	•	Western: Madame Bovary, Crime and Punishment, The Awakening  
	•	Eastern: The Broken Commandment (Japan), Russian Realist Literature  
	•	Themes: Social critique, determinism, psychological depth  
	•	Assignments: Writing Realism: A Modern Interpretation, Character Analysis  
  
📖 UNIT 5: Modernism, Postmodernism & The Future of Literature (Weeks 12-15)  
  
Week 9: Modernism & The Fragmented Self (1900–1950 CE)  
	•	Key Works: The Metamorphosis, The Waste Land, One Hundred Years of Solitude  
	•	Themes: Alienation, stream of consciousness, existential dread  
	•	Assignments: Experimental Narrative Analysis, Identity in Literature  
  
Week 10: Postmodernism & The Breakdown of Meaning (1950–Present)  
	•	Key Works: Beloved, If on a Winter’s Night a Traveler, The Things They Carried  
	•	Themes: Metafiction, irony, unreliable narrators, subjectivity  
	•	Assignments: Media & Literature: How Storytelling Has Changed  
  
Weeks 11-12: Contemporary Global Literature & Digital Storytelling  
	•	Key Works: The Three-Body Problem, Snow Country, The White Tiger  
	•	Themes: Globalization, technology, identity in the modern age  
	•	Assignments: Writing in a Digital Age, Comparing Traditional & Digital Narratives  
  
Weeks 13-15: The Final Research Paper  
	•	Week 13: Research Proposal & Peer Review  
	•	Week 14: Drafting & Instructor Feedback  
	•	Week 15: Final Submission & Reflection
