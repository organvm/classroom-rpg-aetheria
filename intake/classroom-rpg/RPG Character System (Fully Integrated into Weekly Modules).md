	•	Every week, they apply course concepts to their characters:

# 📌 Version 9.4 – Locking in One-Time Purges & Scaling Faction Ritual Penalties  
  
🚀 This iteration finalizes the structure of purges and faction recovery mechanics, ensuring a risk-reward balance.  
  
📜 1. One-Chance Purges—No Second Attempts  
  
📜 Players get only one opportunity to purge their archetype—if they fail, they are locked in for the semester.  
  
✔ How One-Chance Purges Work:  
	•	If a purge is denied, the player remains in their archetype permanently.  
	•	If a purge is sabotaged, the initiator suffers the listed penalties but cannot retry.  
	•	If the purge succeeds, the player fully transforms and assumes the risks of their new form.  
  
📜 Why This Works:  
✔ Ensures that purges are high-stakes and meaningful.  
✔ Prevents players from treating archetypes as temporary.  
✔ Encourages deep commitment to the roles and faction interactions.  
  
📜 2. Faction Ritual Recovery—Escalating Consequences for Multiple Failures  
  
📜 Factions that fail a ritual can retry the following week, but the penalties grow worse with each failure.  
  
✔ How Faction Ritual Recovery Works:  
	•	A faction that fails a ritual must wait one week before retrying.  
	•	Each failure adds an additional penalty, making further attempts riskier.  
	•	After three failures, the faction is permanently locked out of rituals.  
  
✔ Example Scaling Penalties for Repeated Failures:  
	•	1st Failure: The faction is barred from making a proposal for one week.  
	•	2nd Failure: The faction loses a collective skill bonus (ex: weakened persuasion, limited fate influence).  
	•	3rd Failure: The faction is disbanded permanently, forcing its members to scatter or form new alliances.  
  
📜 Why This Works:  
✔ Forces factions to carefully plan their proposals and alliances.  
✔ Prevents the game from stagnating due to reckless ritual attempts.  
✔ Adds a layer of escalating tension and stakes to faction-based gameplay.
