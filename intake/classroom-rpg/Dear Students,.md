Dear Students,  
  
This announcement outlines updates to class expectations, assignments, and attendance policies. These adjustments are designed to create a more structured and productive learning environment.  
  
After reading the information below, submit your required response here before Week 6's Class: Acknowledgment & Reflection Submission  
  
📌 1. Attendance, Technology, & Classroom Conduct  
  
The institution’s policies ensure that classrooms are spaces for focused learning. Disruptions, including off-task technology use and speaking over others, interfere with that goal.  
  
Classroom Expectations:  
  
📌 Raise your hand if you want to contribute. Discussions work when we listen to each other.  
📌 Attendance will be recorded when the lecture and discussion are complete and before independent work time.  
📌 No laptops, phones, or devices during lecture or discussion.  
📌 If you use technology or speak out of turn, I’ll remind you once. If it happens again, I’ll ask you to step out for the session.  
📌 If you’re dismissed, you can still earn attendance by taking the weekly assessment and scoring at least a C (learn more in Section 3 below).  
  
📌 2. Assigned Seating (Self-Selection Begins Week 6)  
  
Starting Week 6, you’ll choose your seat based on how you engage. If you participate regularly, sit toward the center. If you prefer to listen, sit in the outer rows.  
  
✅ Rows 2 & 3: For students who actively contribute to discussions.  
✅ Rows 1 & 4: For students who prefer to observe.  
  
If it looks like your seating choice doesn’t match your level of engagement, I’ll ask you to move.  
  
📌 3. Alternative Assessment Option: "The Analytical Review"  
  
For students who prefer not to attend lectures or discussions, or are unable to follow appropriate classroom conduct, you may take "The Analytical Review," an assessment that reinterprets the week’s content through different perspectives.  
  
Assessment Details:  
  
📌 Handwritten responses only (no devices allowed).  
📌 Technology must be left in the classroom until completion.  
📌 Completion of the assessment = attendance credit (must score a C or higher).  
  
📍 Full details & weekly exam link: The Analytical Review  
  
📌 4. Literary Narrative / Reading Autobiography (Week 6 Shift)  
  
This assignment replaces Week 6’s discussion and close reading. The purpose of this shift is to move away from traditional analysis toward personal engagement with narrative. Rather than only analyzing other texts, you’ll reflect on how storytelling functions in your life.  
  
📍 Full details & submission link: Literary Narrative Essay  
  
📌 5. Weekly Discussion: Catharsis & Reflection  
  
📌 “Through pity and fear, catharsis purifies the soul.” – Aristotle, Poetics  
  
This discussion board is where you can reflect on the course, ask questions, or provide feedback.  
  
📍 Post your thoughts here: Catharsis & Reflection Board  
  
📌 6. Plagiarism, AI-Generated Work, & Revision Policies  
Review Plagiarism & AI-Generated Work announcement.  
Review Revision & Resubmission Policy announcement.  
📌 7. Late Work & Pacing Policy  
✅ Close readings can be submitted the next morning or any of the days following.  
✅ If an assignment does not involve peer interaction, submitting a day or two later is fine.  
❌ If peer engagement is required, the assignment must be submitted on time.  
📌 8. Student Response Requirement & Weekly Journal  
  
To confirm you have read and understood these policies, you will complete a brief response test. The test will consist of fill-in-the-blank and short-answer questions.  
  
📍 Submit your response here: Acknowledgment & Reflection Submission  
  
📌 9. Summary of Key Updates (Effective Next Class)  
  
If you have questions, concerns, or need accommodations, respond with your phone number & availability for a call.  
  
Looking forward to working with you.  
  
Best,  
Professor Anthony Padavano
